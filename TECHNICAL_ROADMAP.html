Section 1

Executive Summary

CrisisLens is a national predictive risk and early warning platform that transforms fragmented environmental data into timely, actionable flood intelligence. By integrating rainfall accumulation, soil moisture, terrain elevation, and historical flood occurrence, CrisisLens provides 3 to 7 days of advance warning before flood peaks — enabling governments, emergency responders, and county authorities to act preventively rather than reactively.

The MVP targets Kenya's Lake Victoria basin, covering 3 counties (Kisumu, Siaya, and Homa Bay), 21 sub-counties, and a combined population of 3.28 million people. The platform delivers role-specific dashboards, an AI-powered analyst briefing panel, a live GIS risk heatmap, and a full alert management workflow — all accessible through a web application backed by a production-ready REST API.

Section 2

Problem Statement

Flood disasters in Kenya's Lake Victoria basin follow a recurring pattern: early signals appear across multiple agencies, remain siloed, and decision-makers respond only after communities are already displaced. This systemic fragmentation turns predictable events into avoidable catastrophes.

The Evidence

  • April–May 2024: Flooding displaced over 40,000 people across Kisumu, Siaya, and Homa Bay. Nyando River overflowed; Lake Victoria rose above seasonal average; Suba South island communities were cut off.
  • Data exists but is siloed: The Kenya Meteorological Department (KMD) collects rainfall data; OCHA maintains historical displacement records; county governments have local knowledge — but none of these systems communicate in real time.
  • Response is reactive: Evacuation orders, relief pre-positioning, and emergency declarations routinely happen after peak flooding — when damage is irreversible.
  • No unified decision-support tool: County officers, national operations centres, and responders work from different datasets, different timelines, and different interfaces.
The CrisisLens insight: The data to predict these floods already exists. The problem is fragmentation — not data scarcity. CrisisLens connects the signals.
Section 3

Solution Overview

CrisisLens functions as a national "risk radar" — continuously aggregating environmental indicators, running a sigmoid-based flood probability model, and distributing role-specific intelligence to the right decision-makers at the right time.

Core Capabilities

Flood Risk Scoring Sigmoid model on 4 inputs: rainfall (mm), soil moisture (0–1), elevation (m), past flood history Probability 0–100%, category (Low/Moderate/High), lead time 3–7 days
GIS Heatmap Sub-county polygons from Kenya GeoJSON, colour-interpolated by flood probability Visual risk heatmap with click-to-drill sub-county detail panel
Role-Based Dashboards JWT role claims route each user to their specific dashboard (4 dashboard types) KPI cards, risk tables, trend charts, alerts feeds tailored per role
AI Analyst Panel GPT-4o-mini with role-aware prompts, county/sub-county context, and conversation history Natural-language risk briefings, evacuation guidance, 1-week outlooks
Alerts Workflow County officers create alerts; responders acknowledge; officers resolve. AuditLog tracks all. Full lifecycle: Active → Acknowledged → Resolved with timestamps
Scenario Simulation Analysts adjust rainfall/moisture/elevation sliders and POST to scoring API What-if probability with delta comparison vs current observed prediction

Flood Scoring Model

The core prediction engine applies a weighted sigmoid function to four observable flood indicators. This approach is interpretable, fast, and produces well-calibrated probabilities without requiring historical training data — making it ideal for an MVP with hardcoded representative values.

InputWeightData SourceExample (Nyando)
Rainfall accumulation (mm)1.2×Kenya Met. Dept. (KMD)165 mm
Soil moisture (0–1)1.4×CHIRPS / Sentinel-1 SAR0.87
Elevation penalty (m AMSL)1.1×SRTM 30m DEM1,134 m → penalty 0.43
Past flood occurrence+0.30OCHA historical dataTrue → +0.30
Output (sigmoid)82% · High · 3-day lead

User Roles & Access Control

Every endpoint is protected with JWT authentication. Role claims are embedded in the access token and enforced by DRF permission classes on each view. county_officer users are additionally scoped to their assigned county at the database query level.

National Ops National Disaster Operations Centre staff National Dashboard All counties, all alerts, KPI overview, trend charts, create alerts for any county
County Officer County Government crisis coordinator County Dashboard Own county only, sub-county risk table, recommended actions, create + resolve alerts

Analyst Data analyst / research officer Analyst Dashboard AI briefing chat, scenario simulation (what-if), prediction history, reports generation

Section 9

Risk & Mitigation

RiskLikelihoodImpactMitigation
OpenAI API unavailable or rate-limited Medium Low Hardcoded fallback response templates already implemented in _fallback_ai_response(). App continues to function fully without AI key.
Live KMD / CHIRPS data feeds inaccessible High Low (MVP) 2024 long-rain season values hardcoded in FLOOD_INDICATORS dict for all 21 sub-counties. Architecture uses source field on FloodObservation to swap live feeds post-MVP.
PostgreSQL local setup friction Low Low Docker Compose with postgres:16-alpine service eliminates all local setup. entrypoint.sh waits for db health before migrating.
Scope creep during 14-day sprint Medium High 47 GitHub issues across 5 milestones define the MVP boundary. SCOPE.md and PLANNING.md are committed to the repository. Epics gate progress.
GeoJSON performance with 21 sub-county polygons Low Low GeoJSON is filtered at parse time to only 3 counties (10 MB → ~500 KB). Leaflet renders this well within browser limits.
Role permission gaps in API Low Medium DRF permission classes tested per role for every endpoint. AuditLog middleware records all write actions for post-hoc review.
Section 10

Post-MVP Roadmap

The MVP architecture is deliberately designed to accept live data feeds and expanded crisis types without structural changes to the API or frontend. The following phases extend the platform progressively after the 14-day MVP is validated.

Phase 2 — Live Data Pipelines Integrate KMD rainfall API, CHIRPS soil moisture, and SRTM elevation into scheduled tasks. Replace hardcoded FLOOD_INDICATORS with real-time observations. Automated nightly prediction runs.
Phase 3 — SMS / WhatsApp Alerts Civilian-facing alert delivery via Africa's Talking SMS gateway and WhatsApp Business API. Civilians in at-risk sub-counties receive alerts in Swahili and English. Public portal option.
Phase 4 — Temporal Forecasting Replace sigmoid scoring with LSTM or Prophet temporal models trained on KMD historical data. Extend lead time to 14 days. Confidence intervals on all predictions.
Phase 5 — Drought Module Activate the existing drought scoring model for Turkana, Marsabit, and Mandera cluster. NDVI stress, price volatility, and historical IPC phase data as inputs. 4–8 week lead time.
Phase 6 — Mobile App React Native mobile app for field responders. Offline-capable map, push notifications for critical alerts, GPS location sharing during evacuations.
Phase 7 — National Scale Expand from 3 counties to all 47 Kenya counties. Multi-hazard risk aggregation. Integration with national disaster management platforms (NDOC, Red Cross).
